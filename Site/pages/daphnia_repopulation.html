What are Daphnia?

Daphnia are a common species of cladoceran that primarily subsist by filterfeeding on phytoplankton. They can be found in nearly any freshwater body of water, and typically range in size from 0.5 to 3 millimeters. Daphnia are remarkable because of two things: Firstly, they exhibit remarkable adaptability, with physical changes to their bodies taking place over a few generations in the presence of predators in order to ensure their survival, and secondly they have the ability to subsist on normally toxic strains of cyanobacteria, allowing them to eat it when other creatures cannot.
