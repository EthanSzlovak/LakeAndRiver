Daphnia Repopulation

Using our new techniques, we have found a way to repopulate native daphnia populations back to healthy levels. This would be beneficial to both the ecosystem on which this project is performed, and to any native fish species. By extension, this would be beneficial for human populations that live on or around the body of water that we are interacting with, by boosting tourist industries such as fishing, sightseeing, and anything else that involves freshwater mariculture.

Algae Bloom Cleanup

Using scientifically proven methods of biomanipulation, we can begin to limit the effects and spread of cyanobacterial blooms in order to prevent them from harming people who live nearby. This would also benefit the native ecosytems, as there are a large variety of deleterious effects that the presence of high amounts of cyanobacteria can inflict on aquatic life.
